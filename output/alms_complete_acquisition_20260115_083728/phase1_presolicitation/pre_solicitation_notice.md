## 2. PROGRAM DESCRIPTION

### 2.1 Overview
The United States Army requires an Advanced Logistics Management System (ALMS) to provide logistics support to 2,800 Army personnel across 15 installations (Program Information, 2026). The Government seeks a cloud-based inventory management platform that delivers real-time visibility of Class IX repair parts, mobile access capabilities for warehouse operations, and seamless integration with existing Army enterprise systems including SAP S/4HANA and Defense Logistics Agency systems (Sources Sought Data, 2026).

The required system must achieve 99.5% availability during duty hours (0600-1800 Local, Mon-Fri) with system response times supporting up to 2,800 concurrent users, while maintaining FedRAMP Moderate security standards and Common Access Card authentication (Sources Sought Data, 2026). Key capabilities include barcode/RFID scanning through mobile applications, predictive analytics for demand forecasting, automated alerts and reporting, and robust data integration (Sources Sought Data, 2026). The Government anticipates a predominantly commercial-off-the-shelf solution with Army-specific customizations, delivered through a Firm Fixed Price contract approach (Program Information, 2026).

### 2.3 Scope of Work
The scope encompasses deployment of the Advanced Logistics Management System across 15 Army installations supporting 2,800 users with scalability to 5,000 users (Program Information, Sources Sought Data, 2026). The contractor shall provide cloud infrastructure with active FedRAMP Moderate authorization, enterprise systems integration with SAP S/4HANA ERP systems and Defense Logistics Agency systems, and Mean Time to Recovery of 2 hours or less (Sources Sought Data, 2026).

Performance locations include Fort Lee, VA and CONUS installations with Initial Operational Capability targeted for June 2026 and Full Operational Capability by December 2026 (Program Information, 2026).
